## APPLICATION STRATEGY

### How to Present Work Samples

- **Audio**: Private SoundCloud or Bandcamp. Unlisted links, no password. Every reviewer already has these apps.
- **Website (alabo.studio)**: Simple `/feedback` page — embedded player, 3 paragraphs, email. No login, no invite code.
- **Never password-protect audio.** Every friction point loses reviewers.
- **Never just attach files.** Many portals have 10-25MB limits. Streaming links are expected.

### Design & Formatting

| Context | Format | Why |
|---------|--------|-----|
| **Grant applications** | Plain text in their portal + PDF attachments | Panels read 50-200 apps. Clear writing, not design. |
| **Label pitches** | Simple email + SoundCloud link | A&Rs listen to 30 seconds. Don't open PDFs. |
| **Festival pitches** | One-pager PDF (clean, minimal) | Curators skim. Canva is OK if minimal template. |
| **Residencies** | Their application form + work samples | Substance over style. |

**No Canva for grant applications.** Fancy layouts signal style over substance. Most grants have specific forms/word limits anyway.

### Social Media

**Not needed.** Zero grants check Instagram. The experimental music / creative technology world runs on: the work, festivals, word of mouth in small committed scenes.

**If you want any presence:**
- Bandcamp page with 1-2 tracks (your storefront — curators check Bandcamp)
- SoundCloud with demo + sketches (for sharing links)
- That's it. No Instagram, no LinkedIn posts needed.

### Framing by Category

| Category | Lead with... |
|----------|-------------|
| **Tech/media art** | Computational/chaos-math angle, software-as-artwork |
| **Music grants** | Compositional framework, tuning systems, performance potential |
| **African diaspora** | Preservation of 19 West African tuning systems, post-colonial music theory |
| **Academic** | Research contribution: computational ethnomusicology, physical modeling |
| **Residencies** | What you need: studio time, technical support, community |

## PRIORITY 1: Creative Capital Award

**Deadline: April 2, 2026 at 3PM ET**
**Amount: Up to $50,000 + advisory support**
**URL: [creative-capital.org/creative-capital-award/award-application](https://creative-capital.org/creative-capital-award/award-application/)**

### Eligibility
- Working artists with 5+ years professional practice
- Not enrolled in a degree program
- No previous Creative Capital Award
- Multidisciplinary, technology, and socially engaged projects welcome in all categories

### Also includes
Through the 2026 applicant pool, recipients will also be selected for the **State of the Art Prize** — $10,000 unrestricted grant to one artist from each US state.

### Angle for Feedback
Lead with: **Multidisciplinary / Technology**. Frame as an artistic instrument practice, not a software product. Emphasize:
- Novel synthesis topology (chaos → waveguide → African tuning)
- Cultural significance (post-colonial instrument design)
- Live performance dimension
- What $50K enables (ethnomusicology advisor, M4L wrapper, demo EP, festival premiere)

### What They Need
- Project description (clear, compelling, non-technical)
- Work samples (audio demo — SoundCloud link)
- Artist statement
- Budget
- Timeline
