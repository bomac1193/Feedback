## Project Title

Feedback: Chaos Resonance Instruments — Completion and First Performance

## Project Summary

Development and public presentation of Feedback, a suite of electronic instruments built in Max/MSP that combine chaos mathematics with physically-modeled resonators tuned to 19 African tonal systems. The grant funds completion of two remaining instruments (a Kuramoto-model rhythm engine and a swarm polyphony engine), a demo recording, and a first live performance showcasing genuinely novel synthesis: sounds produced by strange attractors exciting waveguide resonators through Yoruba, Shona, and Mandinka tuning frameworks.

## Why This Matters

Commercial synthesizers universally encode Western 12-tone equal temperament as default. No electronic instrument offers African tuning systems. Feedback addresses this absence by implementing 19 tuning systems from published ethnomusicological research (Akinbo 2019, Kubik 2010, Tracey 1970, Charry 2000, Arom 1991) as a playable, performable synthesis engine. The result is not "world music" — it is a new sonic territory where the harmonic framework itself carries cultural meaning.
