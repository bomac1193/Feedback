# FEEDBACK — Project Summary

**A live instrument that turns chaos into music through physically-modeled resonators tuned to African tonal systems.**

---

## What It Is

Feedback is a suite of four linked electronic instruments built in Max/MSP that combine chaos mathematics (Lorenz, Rossler, Chua, Halvorsen, Aizawa attractors), waveguide physical modeling, and 19 tuning systems derived from African ethnomusicological research. It produces sounds that don't exist in any acoustic tradition — not a simulation of African instruments, but a new synthesis engine using pitch frameworks from Yoruba, Shona, Mandinka, Chopi, and other musical traditions as its harmonic foundation.

## Why It Matters

Every commercial synthesizer encodes Western 12-tone equal temperament as default. No instrument offers African tuning systems. No instrument combines chaos mathematics with physical modeling for live performance. Feedback addresses both gaps simultaneously, creating a genuine blue ocean in electronic instrument design — positioned at the intersection of computational ethnomusicology, experimental synthesis, and Afrofuturist creative practice.

## What's Built

The primary instrument (P1: Chaos Resonator) is 95% complete and playable now. It includes 5 chaos attractors at audio rate, 6 heterogeneous waveguide resonators, 19 tuning systems with flexible intonation, 9 African and Lusophone rhythm patterns, an arpeggiator with Euclidean rhythm generation, a spring root system, MIDI learn, and 50 curated presets discovered by a custom parameter-sweeping agent. Three additional instruments (rhythm engine, swarm polyphony, corpus resonance) are in development, sharing the same DSP core.
